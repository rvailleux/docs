# the-audio-is-strange

{% hint style="success" %}
The sound quality depends on the device and on the location where the microphone is.
{% endhint %}

What kind of issue do you have?

## I can hear an echo

When there is echo, you can hear yourself in your interlocutor video.

**Why?** Some devices do not erase the audio return. That means that the sound that you emit is transferred to your interlocutor speakers. Then, your interlocutor microphone gets this sound and repeats it back to you.

1. In this case, use a headset. The voice arrives directly into the headset and never gets to the microphone.
2. If you do not have a headset, turn the volume of the speakers down, so the microphone does not get the voice sound.

## There is a strident sound

Sometimes, the microphone makes a strident sound when it is located too close from the speakers.

1. Turn the volume of the speakers down.
2. Move the microphone away from the speakers.

## My voice is drowned by the noise that surrounds me

Sometimes, when the microphone is very close to the mouth, that causes distortion and it becomes very complicated to understand.

On the contrary, when the microphone is too far from the mouth, the sound of the voice can be mixed up with the surrounding noise.

To find the best location of the microphone, make a call test:

1. Before joining a session, click **More** then click **Run call test**.

&#x20;2\. Go to **Recording** tab. 3. Click **Start call test** and record yourself. Do no hesitate to make usual sounds (such as keyboard typing, clics,...) so you can have a preview of what the interlocutor hears.

&#x20;4\. Click **Play** to listen what it sounds like. 5. If the sound does not suit you, run the test again to find a better distance between the microphone and your mouth.
